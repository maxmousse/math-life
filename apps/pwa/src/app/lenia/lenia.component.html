Lenia

Lenia is a continuous cellular automaton that introduces a unique perspective on evolving patterns. Unlike traditional cellular automata, Lenia operates in a continuous space, allowing for a smooth and wave-like evolution of states. This fascinating automaton was developed by Bert Wang-Chak Chan.

In Lenia, each cell can exist in a spectrum of states, creating a more fluid and dynamic landscape. The evolution of the system is governed by differential equations that dictate how the states change over time. This continuous nature gives rise to mesmerizing, organic patterns reminiscent of living organisms.

Lenia is known for its ability to simulate natural processes and produce visually captivating results. Its exploration goes beyond the discrete steps of traditional cellular automata, offering a continuous and expressive canvas for understanding complex dynamics in a computational environment.
